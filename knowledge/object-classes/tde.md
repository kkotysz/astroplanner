# TDE Observing

Summary: Practical guidance for tidal disruption event candidates and other unusual nuclear transients.
Tags: tde, tidal-disruption, agn, transient, practical-observing

## When this note applies

- The target type contains TDE or tidal disruption event.
- The user asks whether a TDE candidate is worth observing tonight.
- The user compares a TDE with SN, AGN, or other transients.

## Key heuristics

- Treat most TDE candidates as faint extragalactic targets: prioritize lower airmass, a stable best window, and reasonable moon separation.
- If the TDE is materially fainter than competing transients, it usually needs a stronger geometric case to be worth choosing tonight.
- Long uninterrupted integrations are generally more valuable than short fragmented looks.

## What to tell the user

- Explain the tradeoff between novelty or interest and actual practical observability tonight.
- Be explicit when the TDE is scientifically interesting but observationally marginal.
- Compare it to other transients using brightness, min airmass, moon separation, and over-limit time.

## Caveats

- TDE labels can appear on candidates with evolving or uncertain classification.
- Source-side priority should not override obviously poor geometry or very faint brightness.
