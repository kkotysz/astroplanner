# Galaxy Observing

Summary: Practical guidance for galaxies and other diffuse extragalactic targets in AstroPlanner.
Tags: galaxy, diffuse-target, practical-observing

## When this note applies

- The target is classified as a galaxy.
- The user asks whether a galaxy is worth attempting tonight.
- The user compares a galaxy with point-like targets under bright-sky conditions.

## Key heuristics

- Diffuse galaxies are penalized harder by moonlight than compact point sources, even when the score is acceptable.
- Min airmass and a high, dark segment of the night matter a lot more for galaxy contrast than they do for bright stellar targets.
- If the galaxy is faint and the Moon is intrusive, a brighter point-source target may be the more practical recommendation.

## What to tell the user

- Explain whether the recommendation is limited by sky brightness, altitude, or both.
- Avoid overpromising visible structure on a small setup if the target is faint or diffuse.
- If the galaxy still makes sense, say why: high altitude, long clean window, or good moon geometry.

## Caveats

- A galaxy can have a good score and still be disappointing if sky brightness is high.
- Bright compact galaxy nuclei are easier than low-surface-brightness structure.
