# Moonlight Tradeoffs

Summary: How to reason about moon separation and bright-sky penalties when suggesting what to observe.
Tags: moonlight, moon, moon-separation, practical-observing

## When this note applies

- The question mentions the Moon, moon separation, or bright sky.
- The target is faint or diffuse and moonlight may matter.
- The user asks whether a target is still worth attempting near the Moon.

## Key heuristics

- A point source can still be practical with moderate moonlight if it remains bright enough and high in the sky.
- Diffuse targets are penalized harder by moonlight than compact point-like targets.
- If the best window drops below the configured moon-separation threshold, frame it as a warning or tradeoff, not necessarily an automatic rejection.

## What to tell the user

- Be explicit whether moonlight is a hard blocker or just a quality penalty.
- Compare moon separation with brightness and min airmass instead of treating it in isolation.
- If a target is still feasible, say what is compromised: contrast, faint structure, or margin for short exposures.

## Caveats

- Current moon separation and best-window moon separation are not the same thing.
- A moon threshold in the UI is a planning aid, not a universal physical cutoff.
